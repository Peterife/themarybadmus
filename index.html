Biography

Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie was born in Enugu, Nigeria in 1977. She grew up on the campus of the University of Nigeria, Nsukka. She is the author of acclaimed novels such as Half of a Yellow Sun and Americanah, as well as the essay collections We Should All Be Feminists and Dear Ijeawele.

Her works have been translated into over thirty languages and have won numerous awards. In addition to fiction, she has delivered influential talks on feminism, race, and identity, inspiring audiences around the world.
